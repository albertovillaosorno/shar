## Question Presented

Which court, law, and conflict-of-laws rules govern each copyright, contract,
anti-circumvention, trademark, patent, trade-secret, privacy, and
platform-policy issue, and when does federal law preempt a state-law claim or
restriction?

## Verified Baseline

GitHub hosting, a United States statute, or a United States resident does not
alone establish one governing law for every claim. Contractual choice-of-law and
forum provisions, personal jurisdiction, territorial conduct, and claim-specific
rules must be analyzed separately.

## Not Established

This record does not establish that United States law controls globally, that a
contract clause is enforceable in every forum, that copyright preemption defeats
all state claims, or that one court's software precedent binds every
jurisdiction.

## Required Facts

- Residence and location of every relevant party and act.
- Hosting, download, development, distribution, and injury locations.
- Exact governing-law, forum, arbitration, and service provisions.
- Claim elements and whether extra contractual or tort elements are alleged.
- Relevant territorial registrations, sales, users, and enforcement activity.

## Required Authorities

- 17 U.S.C. § 301 and controlling preemption authority.
- Applicable federal and state jurisdiction and conflict-of-laws decisions.
- Every operative choice-of-law, forum, and arbitration clause.
- Foreign statutes and controlling authority for material territories.

## Verification Checklist

- Build a claim-by-claim jurisdiction matrix.
- Analyze formation and enforceability of forum provisions separately.
- Identify controlling versus persuasive cases by court and territory.
- Record later history, appeals, statutory amendments, and conflicts.
- Obtain qualified local counsel for every consequential territory.
