## Question Presented

How do the four statutory factors apply to each specific act of copying,
analysis, documentation, testing, display, or distribution?

## Verified Statutory Baseline

Section 107 requires a case-specific assessment of four nonexclusive factors:

1. The purpose and character of the use, including its commercial or nonprofit
   educational nature.
1. The nature of the copyrighted work.
1. The amount and substantiality used in relation to the copyrighted work as a
   whole.
1. The effect of the use on the potential market for, or value of, the
   copyrighted work.

The statutory preamble identifies purposes such as criticism, comment, teaching,
scholarship, and research. A listed purpose may be relevant, but it does not
resolve the analysis or replace consideration of all circumstances.

## Required Analysis

- Evaluate each category of conduct separately rather than treating the project
  as one undifferentiated use.
- Identify the material copied, why it was needed, and whether less copying or a
  non-copying alternative was reasonably available.
- Distinguish intermediate technical copies from material retained in source,
  fixtures, documentation, packages, screenshots, publications, or distributed
  artifacts.
- Evaluate transformation through the actual use and output, not through labels
  such as educational, noncommercial, clean-room, or interoperability.
- Consider traditional, likely, and licensing markets supported by evidence.
- Apply controlling authority from the relevant jurisdiction and record
  materially contrary authority.

## Not Established

- That noncommercial, academic, preservation, or interoperability intent
  guarantees fair use.
- That a clean-room process alone establishes fair use.
- That intermediate copying is always permissible.
- That one favorable software decision controls materially different facts.
- That fair use resolves contract, anti-circumvention, trademark, patent,
  trade-secret, privacy, or platform issues.

## Required Facts

- The purpose, audience, commercial context, and claimed transformation for each
  act.
- The nature and publication status of each copied work.
- The quantity and qualitative importance of the material used.
- The duration and destination of each intermediate or retained copy.
- The content of the final implementation and every distributed output.
- Actual and potential market effects, including relevant licensing markets.
- Available alternatives and the reason the selected amount was necessary.
