# 17 U.S.C. § 109 — Transfer Of A Particular Copy

- Jurisdiction: United States federal copyright law.
- Authority level: Federal statute.
- Statutory currentness: Text contains laws in effect on June 25, 2026.
- As-of date: 2026-07-14.

## Question Presented

What does ownership of a lawfully made physical game copy permit, and does that
ownership itself authorize installation, modification, gameplay streaming, or
distribution of copied material?

## Verified Statutory Baseline

Section 109(a) permits the owner of a particular lawfully made copy to sell or
otherwise dispose of possession of that copy without the copyright owner's
permission. This is the first-sale rule. Section 109(c) permits limited public
display of that particular copy directly or by projecting no more than one image
at a time to viewers present where the copy is located.

Those rules do not provide a general right to reproduce, adapt, transmit, or
publicly perform the work. Section 109(e)'s special public-performance rule is
limited to qualifying games in coin-operated equipment and does not create a
general home-game streaming exception. Ownership of the disc and ownership of
copyright in the software, audiovisual content, music, or characters are
different legal interests.

## Application Boundary

A lawful physical purchase is relevant evidence of ownership of that particular
copy. It can support the ownership element of § 117, but it does not by itself
resolve whether a software transaction is legally an ownership transfer, whether
a proposed adaptation is essential to use, whether contract terms apply, or
whether a video upload is authorized or fair use. In the Ninth Circuit, the
copy-ownership inquiry examines license designation, significant transfer
restrictions, and notable use restrictions; other jurisdictions may use a
different test.

## Not Established

- That first sale authorizes copying game files or distributing a modified
  build.
- That possession of a disc proves ownership of every installed program copy.
- That noncommercial activity is automatically permitted.
- That gameplay transmission falls within § 109(c) or § 109(e).

## Required Evidence

- The physical medium, packaging, receipt, transfer history, and installation
  terms.
- The exact files copied or adapted and the reason for each act.
- The license, access-control, publication, and distribution facts.
- Current controlling authority in the user's jurisdiction.
