## Question Presented

Which Title 17 definitions govern analysis of game code, audiovisual output,
recordings, streams, uploads, downloads, and distributed files?

## Verified Statutory Baseline

Section 101 supplies definitions used throughout Title 17. Classification under
one definition does not by itself establish ownership, infringement, permission,
fair use, or another statutory limitation.

The definitions most material to SHAR distinguish the work from its material
embodiment, fixation from transitory communication, display from performance,
and publication from public performance or display. One technical act may
implicate several definitions and several exclusive rights.

## Definitions Material To SHAR

- An audiovisual work consists of related images intended to be shown by a
  machine or device, together with accompanying sounds when present.
- A computer program is a set of statements or instructions used directly or
  indirectly in a computer to produce a result.
- Copies are material objects in which a work is fixed and from which it can be
  perceived, reproduced, or communicated, directly or with a machine or device.
- A work is fixed when its authorized embodiment is sufficiently stable to be
  perceived, reproduced, or communicated for more than a transitory duration.
  Transmitted sounds or images can be fixed when fixation occurs simultaneously
  with transmission.
- A derivative work recasts, transforms, or adapts one or more preexisting works
  and, as a whole, contains original authorship in its modifications.
- Displaying an audiovisual work means showing individual images
  nonsequentially. Performing it means showing images in any sequence or making
  accompanying sounds audible.
- Publication concerns distribution of copies or phonorecords to the public, or
  an offer to distribute them for further distribution, public performance, or
  public display. Public performance or display alone is not publication.
- A performance or display is public when it occurs in a qualifying public
  place or is transmitted or communicated to the public, even when recipients
  receive it in different places or at different times.
- Transmission means communicating a performance or display so that images or
  sounds are received beyond the place from which they are sent.
- Financial gain includes receiving, or expecting to receive, anything of value.

## Not Established

- That every stream, upload, buffer, cache, recording, or local runtime state is
  a fixed copy, publication, public performance, or public display.
- That every modification, conversion, patch, or generated output is a
  derivative work.
- That a public performance or display necessarily distributes a copy.
- That monetization, attribution, purchase, or noncommercial use determines
  permission, infringement, or fair use.
- That satisfying one definition resolves the separate exclusive-right,
  contract, anti-circumvention, trademark, or platform-policy analysis.

## Required Facts

- The exact code, images, sounds, sequence, metadata, and protected expression.
- Every material object, memory state, recording, cache, and generated output.
- The duration and stability of each embodiment and who authorized fixation.
- Whether images are shown sequentially, sounds are audible, or copies are
  transferred or offered for transfer.
- The audience, recipients, locations, timing, transmission route, and access
  controls.
- Every payment, advertising benefit, subscription, exchange, or expected item
  of value.
- The licenses, ownership evidence, statutory limitations, and defenses relied
  upon.

## Verification Checklist

- Classify the work, material embodiment, fixation, act, and audience separately.
- Distinguish a stream from a downloadable copy and a recording from transient
  playback.
- Distinguish publication from public performance and public display.
- Map each verified definition to the exact exclusive right or limitation being
  analyzed.
- Preserve technical evidence about storage, duration, recipients, and
  transmission.
- Obtain qualified counsel before a consequential copyright conclusion.
