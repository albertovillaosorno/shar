## Question Presented

What protection may exist in original additions to a compilation, mod,
conversion, manifest, or other work using preexisting material, and what is the
effect of unlawful use of that material?

## Verified Statutory Baseline

Section 103(a) recognizes compilations and derivative works as possible
copyright subject matter, but excludes protection from any part in which
preexisting copyrighted material was used unlawfully.

Section 103(b) limits the later author's copyright to contributed material. It
does not grant an exclusive right in the preexisting material and does not alter
that material's scope, duration, ownership, subsistence, or public-domain
status.

The official legislative notes explain that unlawful use prevents protection for
the unlawfully used parts without necessarily eliminating protection from every
independent contribution. They also recognize that use without permission may
still be lawful under fair use or another applicable rule.

## Component Analysis Required

For each output, identify separately:

1. Every preexisting game, mod, format, asset, text, sound, image, or program
   component.
1. The author and chain of title for every new contribution.
1. The authorization, license, statutory limitation, or other lawful-use basis
   for each preexisting component.
1. The original and separable code, metadata, taxonomy, arrangement,
   documentation, or other contribution.
1. The material included in private builds, public source, test fixtures,
   packages, screenshots, and other published or distributed artifacts.
1. Whether enforcement would require asserting control over preexisting material
   rather than the claimant's own contribution.

## Not Established

- That every mod or converted output is unlawful or unprotectable.
- That original additions receive protection merely because effort was spent.
- That technical dependency on a game defeats all rights in independent
  contributions.
- That ownership of new contributions authorizes use or distribution of the
  underlying game material.
- That one contributor's unlawful use removes protection from every separable
  contribution by every author.

## Repository Posture

- Keep game-derived payloads outside Git and distributed artifacts absent an
  artifact-specific rights basis.
- Identify repository-authored code and metadata separately from source content.
- Do not describe the repository license as applying to third-party or
  game-derived material.
- Preserve component-level authorship, provenance, and distribution evidence.
