## Question Presented

What notice, takedown, counter-notice, repeat-infringer, designated-agent,
misrepresentation, privacy, subpoena, restoration, and injunctive-remedy issues
could apply to SHAR material hosted by an online service provider?

## Verified Statutory Structure

Section 512 creates conditional limitations on remedies for qualifying service
providers performing separate functions. It does not decide whether underlying
material infringes, whether a claimant owns the asserted work, or whether a user
has a defense.

An effective notice under § 512(c)(3) requires specified statements and
identification information. Section 512(g) establishes a counter-notice and
replacement process for qualifying subscriber material, including notice to the
subscriber, transmission of the counter-notice to the complaining party, and a
statutory replacement window unless the provider receives notice of a filed
court action.

Section 512(f) creates liability for a knowing material misrepresentation that
material is infringing or that removal resulted from mistake or
misidentification, subject to the statutory elements and injury requirement.

Section 512(i) conditions eligibility on a reasonably implemented repeat-
infringer policy and accommodation of qualifying standard technical measures.
Section 512(h) provides a subpoena procedure for specified identifying
information. Section 512(m) does not generally condition eligibility on active
monitoring or affirmative fact-finding.

## Injunctive-Relief Architecture

Section 512(j) limits the forms of injunctive relief available against a
provider that is not subject to monetary remedies. It directs courts to
consider:

- the burden on the provider and its system or network;
- the magnitude of likely harm to the copyright owner;
- technical feasibility and effectiveness;
- interference with access to noninfringing material; and
- less burdensome, comparably effective alternatives.

This is not a generic platform-immunity rule. It is a conditional remedial
framework that must be analyzed with the provider's function and eligibility.

## SHAR Facts Required

1. The exact hosted file, page, publication, commit, package, and URL.
1. The hosting provider's function and current DMCA policy.
1. The operative notice, claimant, asserted work, signature, statements, and
   location information.
1. The repository operator's ownership, authorization, defenses, and evidence.
1. The complete counter-notice statements and personal information that would be
   disclosed.
1. Account, repeat-infringer, restoration, and litigation history.
1. Applicable platform terms, privacy policy, jurisdiction, and forum.
1. Whether a subpoena, injunction, preservation order, or other proceeding has
   been threatened or filed.

## Not Established

- That GitHub or another host must accept, reject, or restore material.
- That submitting a notice or counter-notice is safe or strategically advisable.
- That a facially complete notice proves infringement or ownership.
- That § 512 immunizes users who upload material.
- That failure to qualify for § 512 proves the provider infringed.
- That platform procedure replaces a merits, privacy, contract, or jurisdiction
  analysis.

## Repository Posture

- Preserve notices, counter-notices, account messages, and affected artifacts
  privately and without alteration.
- Verify chain of title and authorization before making substantive assertions.
- Review personal-information disclosure and litigation risk before any counter-
  notice.
- Treat platform restoration as separate from ultimate legality.
- Obtain qualified counsel before a consequential notice, counter-notice, or
  litigation response.
