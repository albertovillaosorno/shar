# 18 U.S.C. §§ 1836 And 1839 — Trade-Secret Claims

- Status: Current statutory framework verified; application requires exact

- Jurisdiction: United States federal trade-secret law plus applicable state and

- Authority level: Federal statute, state statutes, contracts, and controlling
  cases.
- As-of date: 2026-07-12.
- Counsel review: Not performed.

## Question Presented

Did any information used by SHAR qualify as a trade secret, was it acquired,
used, or disclosed through improper means or breach of duty, and what evidence
supports independent derivation or lawful reverse engineering?

## Verified Baseline

Trade-secret analysis requires the exact information, secrecy measures, economic
value, acquisition method, duties, use, disclosure, and governing law. It is
distinct from copyright and patent analysis.

## Not Established

This record does not establish that public availability defeats every claim,
that all local material was lawfully acquired, that reverse engineering was
contractually permitted, or that independent source code alone proves
independent derivation.

## Required Facts

- Exact asserted information and whether it was generally known or
  ascertainable.
- Measures used to maintain secrecy.
- Acquisition source, access controls, notices, agreements, and confidentiality.
- Exposure matrix for every person or agent and implementation artifact.
- Evidence of independent observation, testing, design, and chronology.

## Required Authorities

- Current 18 U.S.C. §§ 1836 and 1839.
- Applicable state trade-secret statute and controlling cases.
- Exact NDAs, employment agreements, source licenses, and access terms.
- Contemporaneous provenance, design, test, and commit records.

## Verification Checklist

- Identify the asserted information narrowly rather than labeling an entire
  format or repository confidential.
- Build a person-source-output exposure matrix.
- Separate public observations from non-public material.
- Preserve independent hypotheses, failures, tests, and implementation history.
- Obtain qualified counsel before asserting lawful reverse engineering or
  independent derivation.
