## Question Presented

Which exclusive rights are implicated by SHAR's reproduction, intermediate
copying, adaptation, extraction, transformation, display, performance, and
distribution activities for each distinct input and output?

## Verified Baseline

Section 106 defines exclusive rights subject to limitations elsewhere in
Title 17. A technical act can implicate more than one right, and identifying a
right does not decide whether a license, limitation, defense, or exception
applies.

Direct infringement also requires claim-specific attribution of the challenged
act. *Cartoon Network LP, LLLP v. CSC Holdings, Inc.* held that subscribers who
selected programs and issued recording commands caused persistent playback
copies made by an automated remote-DVR system. Designing, hosting, and operating
the system did not itself make the provider the direct copier on that record.
The court expressly did not decide secondary liability.

The same opinion treated transmissions from one subscriber's unique copy to that
subscriber as outside the public-performance right under its narrow transmit-
clause analysis.

*American Broadcasting Companies, Inc. v. Aereo, Inc.* later held that a cable-
like service publicly performed broadcast programs despite subscriber-specific
antennas, copies, one-to-one transmissions, and user initiation. The Supreme
Court treated the service as functionally equivalent to the cable systems that
Congress intended the transmit clause to reach.

*Aereo* described its holding as limited. It did not decide remote-storage DVRs,
cloud storage, reproduction, fixation, or every service hosting user-selected
content. The provider's function, source content, audience, user rights, and
primary service purpose remain necessary.

## Not Established

This record does not establish infringement, ownership, substantial similarity,
a valid registration, distribution, public display, public performance, or the
absence of an applicable limitation or defense.
