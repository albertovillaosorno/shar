## Question Presented

Do SHAR names, descriptions, compatibility statements, domains, package names,
logos, screenshots, or distribution presentation create infringement, false
association, false designation, dilution, or related trademark risk?

## Verified Baseline

Trademark analysis depends on the specific designation, use in commerce,
registration, goods or services, audience, presentation, confusion evidence,
territory, and asserted theory. Copyright interoperability analysis does not
resolve trademark claims.

*Adobe Systems, Inc. v. Christenson* affirmed a Ninth Circuit nominative-use
result where marks identified genuine products rather than suggesting
sponsorship or endorsement. The holding does not make every factual reference
unlikely to confuse consumers and did not decide a false-advertising theory that
was not pleaded.

## Not Established

This record does not establish that factual naming is automatically nominative
fair use, that a disclaimer eliminates confusion, that historical marks are
abandoned, or that noncommercial use defeats every claim.

## Required Facts

- Exact word marks, logos, domains, repository names, screenshots, and metadata.
- Current registrations, owners, licensees, status, classes, and territories.
- Goods, services, audience, commercial context, and channels of distribution.
- Relative prominence, disclaimers, trade dress, and affiliation statements.
- Actual confusion, intent, survey, enforcement, and marketplace evidence.

## Required Authorities

- Current 15 U.S.C. §§ 1114, 1125(a), and 1125(c).
- USPTO TSDR and assignment records for every material United States mark.
- Controlling circuit precedent for confusion, nominative use, and dilution.
- Foreign trademark registers and controlling law for other territories.

## Verification Checklist

- Search exact, related, and historical marks in relevant classes.
- Separate plain-text compatibility references from logos and trade dress.
- Preserve registration and assignment evidence with retrieval dates.
- Test every public presentation rather than only source-code identifiers.
- Obtain qualified trademark counsel before branding or commercial distribution.
