## Question Presented

What rights and obligations arise from hosting SHAR on GitHub, and what legal
questions remain independent of the GitHub-user agreement?

## Verified Baseline

GitHub's terms govern the relationship between GitHub and its users and contain
platform-specific licenses, responsibilities, policies, and a choice-of-law
provision. Other parties' rights, licenses, and contracts remain separate.

The reviewed GitHub Terms of Service are effective 2026-04-27. They distinguish
the user's ownership of submitted content from the licenses granted to GitHub,
its affiliates, and other users. The service license covers storing, hosting,
archiving, parsing, displaying, and copying submitted content as needed to
provide, develop, and improve the service. The current text expressly includes
training GitHub AI features and developing or improving artificial-intelligence
and machine-learning models and technologies of GitHub affiliates.

For public repositories, the terms grant other GitHub users a nonexclusive,
worldwide license to use, display, perform, and reproduce submitted content by
forking it through GitHub's functionality. A contribution to a repository that
contains a license notice is licensed under that repository license unless a
separate agreement controls. These platform permissions do not replace SHAR's
outbound license, cure a contributor's lack of authority, or establish rights in
third-party material uploaded by a user.

The agreement incorporates other operating rules and policies and points to
additional product terms for features such as Actions, Packages, and Pages. It
also contains separate rules for AI-feature inputs and outputs, including an
individual-account opt-out that does not limit the broader repository-content
licenses. Repository visibility, selected features, account type, accepted terms
version, account settings, and any enterprise or volume agreement therefore
determine the relevant platform contract set.

## Not Established

- That GitHub hosting makes United States law control every dispute.
- That a public repository grants every off-platform reuse right.
- That GitHub's terms invalidate third-party licenses or restrictions.
- That a platform DMCA process decides ultimate liability.

## Required Facts

- The account type and exact terms accepted.
- Repository visibility, license notice, contributor authority, and content.
- Additional product, API, automation, marketplace, or organization terms.
- The parties and claims in the actual dispute.
