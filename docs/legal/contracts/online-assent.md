# Browsewrap, Clickwrap, Sign-In-Wrap, And Online Assent

## Question Presented

Did the relevant user receive legally sufficient notice and manifest assent to
the specific terms, and are the asserted restrictions enforceable and applicable
to the exact conduct?

## Verified Baseline

Online contract formation commonly requires adequate notice and a manifestation
of assent under the governing state law. Interface labels such as browsewrap,
clickwrap, sign-in-wrap, and shrink-wrap describe presentation patterns but do
not decide the legal result.

In *ProCD, Inc. v. Zeidenberg*, the Seventh Circuit enforced terms presented
inside a retail software package where the package gave notice, the software
displayed the terms, the buyer could return the product, and the buyer used it
instead of rejecting it. The court applied Wisconsin's Uniform Commercial Code
and did not establish that every post-purchase term is enforceable.

In *Nguyen v. Barnes & Noble*, the Ninth Circuit declined to enforce terms where
the user lacked actual notice and the interface did not require affirmative
acknowledgement, even though terms hyperlinks appeared near transaction buttons.

In *Berman v. Freedom Financial Network*, the Ninth Circuit stated that inquiry-
notice enforcement requires both reasonably conspicuous notice and a user action
that unambiguously manifests assent. The challenged visual presentation and
continue buttons did not satisfy those requirements.

In *Specht v. Netscape*, the Second Circuit, applying California formation law,
held that a reasonably prudent user would not have learned of license terms
placed below the download button, so downloading alone did not manifest assent,
and affirmed denial of the motion to compel arbitration. The opinion text was
verified from a permitted secondary duplicate; an official authenticated copy
was not located in the reviewed official repositories, and later controlling
Second Circuit treatment has not been established here.

## Formation Evidence Required

For every relevant agreement, preserve:

1. The complete page, dialog, installer, account, or transaction sequence.
1. The location, wording, color, size, contrast, and visibility of the notice.
1. The exact link text and complete terms version.
1. The user action alleged to manifest assent.
1. Whether the user had actual knowledge from another source.
1. The date, account state, product surface, and transaction context.
1. Governing law, forum, arbitration, modification, and incorporation clauses.
1. Evidence of later amendments and any required renewed assent.

## Separate Questions After Formation

Even when formation is established, analyze separately:

- Which party is bound and which conduct falls within the agreement.
- Whether a term is a covenant, a condition, a restriction on access, or a
  restriction on use.
- Whether the term is unconscionable, preempted, waived, modified, or contrary
  to applicable nonwaivable law or public policy.
- The available contract remedy and whether copyright or DMCA elements are also
  satisfied.
- Territorial, consumer, capacity, and choice-of-law limitations.

## Not Established

- That public-page or browsewrap terms are always unenforceable.
- That clickwrap or sign-in-wrap terms are always enforceable.
- That absence of a signed NDA means no contract or confidentiality duty exists.
- That public repository access eliminates agreed restrictions.
- That statutory interoperability provisions automatically invalidate or preempt
  every private restriction.
- That one circuit's online-assent rules control every transaction.
