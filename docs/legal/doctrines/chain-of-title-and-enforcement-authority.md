## Question Presented

Who currently owns or controls the rights relevant to each game, middleware,
format, trademark, recording, translation, mod, tool, and document, and what
evidence establishes authority to license or enforce those rights?

## Verified Baseline

Historical developer and publisher credits do not establish present ownership of
every component. Copyright, trademark, patent, contract, and corporate ownership
may follow different chains.

Public corporate evidence establishes only a bounded chronology:

- Activision first-party material identifies *The Simpsons: Hit & Run* among
  Radical Entertainment titles.
- Activision Blizzard's 2009 and 2010 Form 10-K filings describe the 2008
  combination of Activision and Vivendi Games and refer to acquisition-related
  consideration concerning Radical Entertainment.
- Microsoft announced completion of its Activision Blizzard acquisition on
  2023-10-13; later Microsoft material identifies Activision Blizzard as a
  wholly owned subsidiary.
- Disney announced that its acquisition of Twenty-First Century Fox became
  effective on 2019-03-20.

That chronology does not establish which entity owns each item of game code,
artwork, music, performance, localization, middleware, trademark, or contract
right. The game-specific licenses, schedules, retained-rights provisions, and
recorded transfers needed for a complete component chain are not present in the
reviewed record.

## Not Established

This record does not establish current ownership from a historical copyright
notice, acquisition announcement, repository username, uploader identity,
registration alone, or possession of a copy.

## Required Facts

- Work or asset identity, authors, employment status, and creation date.
- Written assignments, licenses, mergers, acquisitions, dissolutions, and
  retained-rights provisions.
- Copyright and trademark registrations and current recorded owners.
- Publisher, platform, music, performance, middleware, and localization rights.
- Territory, duration, exclusivity, sublicensing, and enforcement authority.

## Required Authorities

- 17 U.S.C. §§ 201, 204, and 205 from the U.S. House.
- U.S. Copyright Office registration and recordation records.
- USPTO TSDR and assignment records for material marks.
- SEC, corporate-registry, merger, and acquisition records.
- Signed agreements or authoritative first-party statements.

## Verification Checklist

- Build a separate chain for every material component and right type.
- Preserve document dates, parties, territories, and operative grant language.
- Distinguish ownership from an exclusive license and enforcement standing.
- Record unresolved gaps rather than inferring a favorable successor.
- Obtain qualified counsel before relying on a chain-of-title conclusion.
