## Question Presented

Does any repository input, manifest, log, path, account metadata, video
metadata, mod package, generated report, or research evidence contain personal
data or other regulated information, and what collection, storage, publication,
retention, security, or deletion duties apply?

## Verified Baseline

Technical metadata can identify a person or device even when it is not labeled
personal. Public availability does not eliminate privacy, publicity, security,
or platform obligations.

## Not Established

This record does not establish that hashes are anonymous, that usernames are
non-personal, that public profiles may be republished without limit, or that a
local research file is outside data-protection law.

## Required Facts

- Data-field inventory, source, purpose, recipients, and retention period.
- Data-subject location and whether children or sensitive data are involved.
- Account names, email addresses, paths, identifiers, IP addresses, and logs.
- Legal basis, consent, notice, contractual terms, and user expectations.
- Security controls, access, deletion, backups, and publication surface.

## Required Authorities

- Applicable federal, state, national, and regional privacy statutes.
- Platform privacy policies and data-processing terms.
- Repository, publication, and distribution data inventories.
- Written consent or authorization where relied upon.

## Verification Checklist

- Minimize collection and publication to necessary factual evidence.
- Redact machine paths, account identifiers, and unnecessary personal details.
- Separate public citation metadata from private chain-of-custody evidence.
- Define retention and deletion rules before collecting additional data.
- Obtain qualified privacy counsel for regulated or cross-border processing.
