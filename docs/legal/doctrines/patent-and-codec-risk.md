## Question Presented

Do any active patents, patent licenses, pool terms, or standards commitments
apply to codecs, compression methods, media containers, graphics compression, or
other technical methods implemented, invoked, or distributed by SHAR?

## Verified Baseline

Copyright and open-source licenses do not resolve patent infringement. A codec
may have an open implementation while particular claims, territories, uses, or
encoders remain subject to patent analysis.

## Not Established

This record does not establish that HAP, PCM, PNG, DDS compression, FFmpeg
features, FBX, or any proprietary game format is patent-free, licensed, expired,
or enforceable in every territory.

## Required Facts

- Exact algorithms, profiles, features, encoders, decoders, and build flags
  used.
- Countries of manufacture, use, hosting, sale, and distribution.
- Active patent claims, expiration, ownership, assignments, and licensing
  status.
- Standards-body declarations, patent pools, covenants, and field-of-use limits.
- Whether SHAR implements, links, invokes, or merely processes the technology.

## Required Authorities

- USPTO Patent Center and Assignment Center records.
- WIPO PATENTSCOPE and relevant national patent registers.
- Official standards-body patent declarations and patent-pool licenses.
- Upstream project patent notices and exact build documentation.

## Verification Checklist

- Search by technology, assignee, inventor, classification, and cited patents.
- Read claims and prosecution history; do not rely on titles or abstracts alone.
- Confirm current legal status and territory for every material claim.
- Separate implementation risk from content-licensing and copyright questions.
- Obtain qualified patent counsel before commercial or broad distribution.
