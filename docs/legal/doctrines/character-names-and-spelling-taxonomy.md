# Character Names And Spelling Taxonomy

> [Legal Research Disclaimer](../disclaimer.md) applies to this record.

- Status: Evidence recorded; application remains fact-specific.
- Jurisdiction: United States federal law and European Union law; every
  distribution territory requires separate review.
- Authority level: U.S. Copyright Office practice, federal trademark statutes,
  Court of Justice of the European Union authority, and fact-specific cases.
- As-of date: 2026-07-13.
- Counsel review: Not performed.

## Question Presented

May SHAR place independently verified character names in the repository's
named-entity spelling wordlist when those names are used only as factual
identifiers for clean-room interoperability research, metadata classification,
and documentation?

## Verified Baseline

The U.S. Copyright Office Compendium states that words and short phrases such as
names, titles, and slogans are not copyrightable because they contain a de
minimis amount of authorship. Its examples expressly include the name of a
character. This is a U.S. registration and copyrightability baseline; it does
not decide trademark, unfair-competition, contract, database, or presentation
questions.

European Union law should not be summarized as a categorical short-text
exemption. In *Infopaq*, the Court of Justice stated that words considered in
isolation are not themselves the author's intellectual creation, while the
choice, sequence, and combination of words may express protected originality.
An extract of 11 words could therefore be a protected reproduction when it
contains such original expression. A list of isolated names must be evaluated
separately from copied dialogue, descriptions, catchphrases, or expressive
sequences.

Trademark analysis remains distinct. A character name may function as a mark or
source identifier, and nominative or descriptive-use doctrines are
fact-specific. Internal technical reference does not automatically establish
lawful public branding, commercial presentation, package naming, domain use, or
publication or distribution metadata.

## Repository Application

SHAR classifies a verified character name in
`docs/cspell/named-entities.txt` as named-entity metadata when all of the
following are true:

1. The spelling is independently verified from an identified reliable source.
1. The entry is an isolated name used as a factual identifier.
1. The name is needed in repository-authored interoperability documentation,
   tests, schemas, or metadata classification.
1. The entry does not copy character art, biography text, dialogue, a
   catchphrase, narrative description, or another expressive passage.
1. The source is not copied wholesale into a database-like catalog.
1. The wordlist entry is not presented as affiliation, sponsorship,
   endorsement, licensing, or official status.

The wordlist should not admit asset IDs, random identifiers, usernames,
malformed strings, copied descriptive text, or synthetic fixtures merely because
they originate in third-party material. Those sequences require exact local
suppression or separate evidentiary treatment.

This is an editorial and engineering classification rule. It does not determine
rights to use a name in a product title, logo, domain, marketing surface,
downloadable package, or commercial distribution.

## Scope Of The Recorded Analysis

- Under the cited U.S. Copyright Office practice, an isolated character name is
  a word or short phrase; the exact list may still require separate selection
  and arrangement analysis.
- Under *Infopaq*, EU originality and reproduction analysis depends on whether
  the selected material expresses the author's intellectual creation; isolated
  words and copied expressive sequences are not equivalent facts.
- Trademark infringement and nominative-use questions depend on territory,
  source-identifying use, presentation, likely confusion, and the applicable
  statutory and judicial tests.
- Compilation, database, contract, and source-acquisition questions depend on
  how the list was assembled and which source supplied it.
- Product-owner policies and licenses are separate contractual or permission
  layers and must be checked for the publication or distribution surface to
  which they apply.

## Not Established

- That an entire character, characterization, visual design, biography, or
  dialogue is unprotected because the name alone is short.
- That every list of names lacks protectable selection or arrangement.
- That factual necessity automatically establishes fair use or nominative use.
- That noncommercial, internal, or interoperability intent resolves trademark,
  contract, database, trade-secret, or platform-policy questions.
- That inclusion in a spelling dictionary authorizes branding or distribution.

## Required Facts

- The exact names and source used to verify each spelling.
- Whether the source list was copied, independently assembled, or inferred from
  lawful local input.
- The repository surfaces and audiences in which each name appears.
- Whether names appear alone or with expressive descriptions, art, dialogue, or
  ordered narrative material.
- The commercial context, distribution territories, presentation, and
  disclaimers.
- Current trademark registrations, owners, licensees, and relevant classes.

## Verification Checklist

- Verify every name and preserve its source.
- Review the complete source list for copied selection or arrangement.
- Separate isolated names from expressive descriptions and sequences.
- Determine the controlling trademark doctrine for every distribution
  territory.
- Recheck current registrations and product-owner policies.
- Obtain qualified counsel before public branding or commercial distribution.
