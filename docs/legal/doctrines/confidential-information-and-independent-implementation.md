## Question Presented

What evidence and engineering separation are required to demonstrate that SHAR
was implemented independently from non-public third-party source, confidential
information, or material acquired through an improper means?

## Verified Baseline

Trade-secret analysis is distinct from copyright. Public availability,
functional necessity, reverse engineering, independent derivation, contractual
confidentiality, and improper acquisition must be evaluated under the governing
law and actual evidence.

## Not Established

This record does not establish that all local reference material was public,
that reverse engineering is permitted by every contract, that source-expression
separation alone defeats a trade-secret claim, or that calling a workflow
clean-room makes it legally sufficient.

## Required Facts

- Acquisition source, date, lawful-access basis, and confidentiality markings.
- Exact people or agents exposed to each source and implementation artifact.
- Whether any NDA, employment duty, access restriction, or source license
  applied.
- Design notes, test evidence, commit chronology, and independent
  specifications.
- Any copied names, constants, comments, layouts, algorithms, or implementation
  choices that are not functionally required.

## Required Authorities

- 18 U.S.C. §§ 1836 and 1839 from the U.S. House.
- Applicable state trade-secret statute and controlling cases.
- Every NDA, employment agreement, source license, and access term.
- Contemporaneous provenance and implementation records.

## Verification Checklist

- Build an exposure matrix by person, source, date, and output.
- Separate public observations from non-public reference material.
- Identify every potentially copied expressive or nonfunctional choice.
- Preserve independent tests, failed hypotheses, and design chronology.
- Obtain qualified counsel before relying on an independent-implementation
  defense.
