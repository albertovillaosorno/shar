## Question Presented

When may SHAR use game, franchise, format, vendor, tool, and compatibility names
to identify factual interoperability without creating infringement, dilution,
false association, sponsorship, endorsement, or passing-off risk?

## Verified Baseline

Trademark analysis is distinct from copyright and contract. Accurate reference
to a compatibility target does not by itself resolve likelihood of confusion,
commercial presentation, prominence, disclaimers, domain names, logos, trade
dress, or territorial rights.

The title *The Simpsons: Hit & Run* and the THE SIMPSONS designation are
material compatibility references for this repository. Historical USPTO Official
Gazette material records a THE SIMPSONS application by Twentieth Century Fox
Film Corporation. Current Disney+ and FOX consumer pages use the designation for
the television series.

Those sources establish historical application evidence and current first-party
commercial use. They do not establish that one federal registration is active,
identify the current owner of every relevant registration, prove an assignment
chain, or resolve rights in every territory, product class, game title, logo, or
other presentation.

The accepted repository decision is narrower than a legal conclusion: use
plain-text factual compatibility wording, keep SHAR's own identity prominent,
omit third-party logos and trade dress, and do not imply affiliation,
sponsorship, endorsement, approval, or current official support.

## Not Established

This record does not establish that nominative use is automatically lawful, that
a disclaimer cures confusion, that historical names are abandoned, or that a
repository name may reproduce third-party branding without review.

## Required Facts

- Exact words, logos, screenshots, domains, package names, and metadata used.
- Commercial or noncommercial context and intended audience.
- Current registrations, owners, licensees, and relevant territories.
- Relative prominence of SHAR branding and compatibility references.
- Evidence of actual confusion, endorsement claims, or affiliation statements.

## Required Authorities

- 15 U.S.C. §§ 1114, 1125, and 1125(c) from the U.S. House.
- Current USPTO TSDR records for every material mark.
- Controlling circuit precedent for nominative and descriptive use.
- Product-owner branding and fan-content policies, if any.
