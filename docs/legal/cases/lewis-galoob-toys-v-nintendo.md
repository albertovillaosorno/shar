# Lewis Galoob Toys, Inc. v. Nintendo of America, Inc

> [Legal Research Disclaimer](../disclaimer.md) applies to this record.

- Status: Reported Ninth Circuit opinion text verified from a public-domain
  transcription.
- Court: United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit.
- Authority: 964 F.2d 965 (9th Cir. 1992).
- Decision date: 1992-05-21; amended 1992-08-05.
- As-of date: 2026-07-13.
- Counsel review: Not performed.

## Question Presented

Did a consumer accessory that temporarily changed values passing from a game
cartridge to a console create infringing derivative works or contributory
liability?

## Verified Holding And Record

The court affirmed judgment for the accessory distributor. The device blocked
and replaced selected data-byte values while a game ran, did not alter the data
stored in the cartridge, and produced only temporary effects. The court held
that the resulting displays did not incorporate the protected game in a concrete
or permanent form and therefore were not derivative works on the record before
it.

The court also upheld the district court's alternative fair-use conclusion. It
focused on consumers' private, nonprofit home use, the need to own or obtain the
original game, and the absence of proven present or reasonably likely market
harm. Because the consumer conduct was not infringing, marketing the accessory
did not establish the asserted contributory or authorization liability.

## Limits And Later Treatment

- The holding depended on temporary runtime changes, no alteration of the game
  cartridge, no independently generated display, and no substitution for the
  original game.
- The decision does not establish a general right to copy, distribute, sell, or
  publish modified game files or protected audiovisual material.
- *Micro Star v. FormGen, Inc.* later distinguished fixed level-description
  files and characterized *Galoob*'s fair-use analysis as unnecessary to its
  derivative-work holding.
- The decision predates the DMCA and does not decide access-control,
  anti-trafficking, contract, or current platform-policy questions.
- Fair use remains fact-specific, including purpose, material used, and actual
  or potential market effects.

## Repository Relevance

A purely local runtime enhancement that requires the user's own game copy,
changes only transient state, preserves the original files, and distributes no
protected output is closer to the facts in *Galoob* than a fixed commercial mod
package. That comparison is evidence, not a categorical legal conclusion. SHAR
must separately analyze fixation, copied expression, distribution, licenses,
contracts, and Section 1201.
