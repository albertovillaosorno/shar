## Question Presented

Can original selection, coordination, or arrangement remain protectable when a
form or data presentation also contains standard, functional, or constrained
material?

## Verified Holding And Record

The court affirmed an infringement judgment involving forms used in an
administrative business process. It rejected the categorical proposition that
industry standards, functional subject matter, or unprotectable individual
components necessarily eliminate protection from an original selection,
coordination, or arrangement.

The opinion provides an important counterweight to overbroad merger and scenes-
a-faire arguments. Protection may be thin and limited to the original expressive
combination, but functional constraints do not automatically end the inquiry.

## Limits

- Protection in selection or arrangement does not create ownership of underlying
  facts, systems, methods, standard fields, or necessary interfaces.
- The scope of protection and substantial-similarity analysis remain narrow and
  fact-specific.
- The decision does not establish that every schema, manifest, screen, or format
  arrangement is sufficiently original.

## Repository Relevance

A SHAR format analysis must evaluate both sides of the filtration inquiry:
functional and standard elements may be excluded, while an independently
original selection or arrangement may remain protectable. A conclusion cannot be
based only on the functional purpose of the artifact.
