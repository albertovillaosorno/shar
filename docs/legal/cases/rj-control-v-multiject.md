## Question Presented

How did the Sixth Circuit apply originality, merger, and scenes-a-faire analysis
to software source code shaped by hardware, compatibility, and industry
constraints?

## Verified Holding And Record

The court affirmed summary judgment after reviewing the alleged copied portions
and the functional constraints identified in the record. It applied *Lexmark*'s
software-copyright framework and treated efficiency, hardware specifications,
compatibility requirements, programming practices, and industry conventions as
potential constraints on expressive choice.

The court did not hold that software is generally unprotectable. It required a
concrete comparison of the asserted expression and the constraints affecting
that expression.

## Limits

- Functional constraint is an evidentiary question; it cannot be inferred merely
  from the fact that code operates hardware or follows a protocol.
- Merger and scenes a faire do not eliminate protection from independently
  original unconstrained expression.
- The result depends on the identified code, expert evidence, procedural record,
  and governing circuit law.

## Repository Relevance

When assessing a parser, serializer, compatibility table, or command grammar,
SHAR should document which choices are dictated by observed input, standards,
interoperability, efficiency, or platform behavior and which choices remain
independently expressive.
