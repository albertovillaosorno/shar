- As-of date: 2026-07-12.
- Counsel review: Not performed.

## Question Presented

When can a user be bound to online terms through inquiry notice rather than an
express checkbox or equivalent acknowledgement?

## Verified Holding And Record

The court identified two conditions for enforcing online terms through inquiry
notice: the website must provide reasonably conspicuous notice of the terms, and
the user must take an action that unambiguously manifests assent. The challenged
pages did not satisfy those requirements because the notice was visually
inconspicuous and the continue buttons did not clearly communicate agreement to
the terms.

The opinion distinguished labels such as clickwrap, browsewrap, and sign-in-wrap
from the underlying contract-formation inquiry.

## Limits

- The decision does not establish one universally sufficient font, color,
  placement, button label, or interface design.
- It does not eliminate actual-knowledge theories.
- It applies Ninth Circuit contract doctrine and must be combined with governing
  state law and later controlling authority.
- Enforceability, scope, unconscionability, preemption, and breach remain
  separate questions after formation.

## Repository Relevance

SHAR must preserve screenshots or equivalent evidence of the complete assent
flow, including the terms link, wording, visual hierarchy, required action,
account state, and operative terms version. A user action is probative only when
the interface objectively connects that action to agreement.
