# Nguyen v. Barnes & Noble, Inc

> [Legal Research Disclaimer](../disclaimer.md) applies to this record.

- Status: Primary Ninth Circuit opinion verified.
- Court: United States Court of Appeals for the Ninth Circuit.
- Authority: 763 F.3d 1171 (9th Cir. 2014).
- Decision date: 2014-08-18.
- As-of date: 2026-07-12.
- Counsel review: Not performed.

## Question Presented

Did a website user receive sufficient notice and manifest assent to terms linked
through a browsewrap presentation?

## Verified Holding And Record

The court declined to enforce the arbitration provision because the record did
not establish actual notice and the website design did not provide sufficient
constructive notice. A conspicuous hyperlink placed near transaction buttons was
not enough where the user was not required to affirmatively acknowledge the
terms and the surrounding interface did not prompt assent.

The opinion applied state contract-formation principles and emphasized that
online labels do not replace ordinary notice and assent analysis.

## Limits

- The decision does not make every browsewrap presentation unenforceable.
- It does not make every clickwrap or sign-in-wrap presentation enforceable.
- The result depends on the interface, wording, placement, user action, actual
  knowledge, governing law, and complete transaction record.
- Later cases may refine the relevant jurisdiction's standard.

## Repository Relevance

For every external download, account, or hosted service relied upon by SHAR, the
research record should preserve the exact terms version and the interface shown
before access. A terms hyperlink or public availability alone does not prove
assent or the absence of a contract.
