# Public automation and maintenance boundary

- Status: Accepted
- Decision date: 2026-07-12
- Scope: Public source use and maintenance expectations

## Context

Public source may be read by humans and machines, but publication does not erase
third-party rights or create a permanent service obligation.

## Decision

Repository-owned material may be lawfully retrieved, indexed, analyzed, and used
for automated code generation under its license. Human and machine output share
the same provenance, security, correctness, attribution, and validation duties.

The repository provides no maintenance schedule, response-time promise,
compatibility window, issue-triage guarantee, or permanent availability
commitment.

## Consequences

- Machine authorship is neither an exemption nor a defect.
- Third-party material remains governed by its owners.
- Issues may remain unanswered and the repository may be archived.

## Rejected alternatives

- Human authorship as evidence of correctness.
- Public availability as unrestricted permission or support contract.
