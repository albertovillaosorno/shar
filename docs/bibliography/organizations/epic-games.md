## Covered Material

Epic Games, Inc. is the organization covered by this record. Epic's official
company page identifies it as a United States company founded in 1991,
headquartered in Cary, North Carolina, and active in interactive entertainment
and real-time 3D engine technology.

This record covers organization identity and the relationship between Epic and
Epic-issued products or agreements relevant to SHAR. It does not consolidate
Unreal Engine, Epic Games Store, Epic Online Services, Epic Games Accounts,
Fortnite, UEFN, Fab, marketplace content, Bink, plugins, or other products into
one license or rights statement.

## Repository Use And Scope

SHAR uses Unreal Engine and Epic-provided engine modules, editor capabilities,
documentation, and project-generation tooling under the applicable Unreal Engine
agreement. Organization-level attribution and source routing are recorded here;
engine-specific technical and license evidence remains in the
[Unreal Engine record](../engine-and-plugins/unreal-engine.md).

No first-class Epic Games Store, Epic Online Services, Epic Games Account,
Fortnite, or UEFN integration was identified in the reviewed repository
evidence. Generated project files may contain `EpicGames.*` build-system names
because they reference installed Unreal Engine tooling; those names do not
establish a separate Epic service integration.

## Authorship, Ownership, And Attribution

Epic Games, Inc., its affiliates, contributors, and licensors retain applicable
rights in their software, services, documentation, content, and marks. This
record does not determine ownership of every Epic product or component and does
not treat organization identity as proof that one agreement governs all Epic
materials.

SHAR uses the names `Epic Games` and `Unreal Engine` only for factual,
nominative identification. The repository does not represent affiliation,
sponsorship, endorsement, certification, partnership, or approval by Epic.

## License Or Terms Basis

Epic's agreements are product-specific. The Epic Games Terms of Service states
that it does not apply to Unreal Engine, the Epic Games Store, or Epic
marketplaces and directs users to the applicable terms for those products.
Unreal Engine is governed separately by the Unreal Engine End User License
Agreement and any applicable additional terms and installed third-party
notices.

The Fan Content Policy is a limited policy for qualifying fan content accessed
under applicable Epic agreements. It is not a general license for SHAR,
Unreal Engine technology, Epic marks, or third-party content and does not
replace product-specific agreements.
