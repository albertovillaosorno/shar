# Xbox Game Content Usage Rules

This non-governing record documents Microsoft's public Xbox Game Content Usage
Rules as a source for creator-content and monetization analysis. It does not
determine whether the rules cover the game studied by SHAR, authorize SHAR, or
grant rights in content owned or licensed by another party.

## Covered Material

The reviewed page is titled *Game Content Usage Rules* and states `Last Updated:
January 2015`. It describes a conditional, personal, non-exclusive,
non-sublicensable, non-transferable, revocable, limited license for qualifying
items made from covered game content.

The page limits coverage to games and game content published and owned by
Microsoft Studios where Microsoft owns the relevant copyright, trademark, or
other intellectual-property rights. It expressly states that Microsoft cannot
grant permission for games from other publishers or for content that Microsoft
does not own.

## Repository Use And Scope

SHAR uses this record only as supporting evidence for the separate legal
analysis
of gameplay videos, streaming, and monetization. The record does not establish
that the rules cover *The Simpsons: Hit & Run*, any licensed franchise element,
music, performance, artwork, or other third-party content in that game.

The rules also prohibit reverse engineering a covered Microsoft game to access
assets or perform actions the game does not normally permit in order to create a
creator item. The policy therefore cannot be treated as authorization for the
SHAR pipeline, extraction, conversion, interoperability research, or local game
modification.

## Provenance And Version History

The official Xbox page remained publicly accessible on the access date and
identified January 2015 as its last update. No separate version identifier,
archived change history, or game-specific coverage list was present in the
reviewed page text.

A live page with an old stated update date is current publication evidence, not
proof that every product, right, or later acquisition falls within its scope.
Consequential creator publication requires a fresh check of the live page and
the exact game's publisher and rights-holder policy.

## Authorship, Ownership, And Attribution

Microsoft is the publisher of the reviewed policy. Microsoft and relevant third
parties retain their rights in covered games, content, marks, music, and other
materials. The policy does not transfer ownership to creators.

For shared creator items, the rules require a readily discoverable notice that
identifies the Microsoft game, states that the item was created under the Game
Content Usage Rules, disclaims endorsement or affiliation, and links to the
rules. That notice applies only when the policy actually covers the item.

## License Or Terms Basis

The reviewed policy states that its limited license is conditional and
revocable. It permits qualifying items to participate in advertising-revenue
programs on YouTube or Twitch, while generally prohibiting sale or other
compensation except where the rules expressly allow it.

The policy separately warns that soundtracks, audio effects, brands, logos, and
other content may be licensed from third parties and may require additional
permission. A corporate acquisition, publishing relationship, or platform label
is not enough to prove that Microsoft owns every right embodied in a game.

## Distribution, Modification, And Compatibility

This record does not authorize distribution of game files, extracted assets,
modified executables, replacement builds, access-control material, or any other
proprietary payload. It also does not establish publisher permission for
gameplay
footage from a game outside the policy's covered-content definition.

Any compatibility or creator-policy statement must identify the exact game,
rights holder, policy version or access date, content used, monetization method,
and required notice. Third-party music and franchise content require separate
review.

## Compliance Posture

- Treat the covered-content definition as a threshold condition.
- Record the live access date and the page's January 2015 update statement.
- Preserve the required notice and policy link only for qualifying items.
- Do not treat YouTube or Twitch advertising permission as a general commercial
  license.
- Do not use the policy as authorization for reverse engineering, extraction,
  conversion, or redistribution.
- Re-review game ownership, publishing rights, music, trademarks, and other
  third-party rights for each creator publication.
