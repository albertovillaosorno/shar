# YouTube Game Content And Monetization Policies

This non-governing record documents official YouTube policy pages relevant to
gameplay-video monetization and channel-level originality review. It does not
determine copyright infringement, fair use, publisher permission, or eligibility
for the YouTube Partner Program in a specific case.

## Covered Material

The official *Video game and software content* page states that monetization may
depend on commercial-use rights granted by the video-game publisher. Without an
appropriate publisher license, it says game use must be minimal unless
step-by-step commentary is strictly tied to the live action and provides
instructional or educational value. It also states that extended videos simply
showing a user playing may not be accepted for monetization.

The official *YouTube channel monetization policies* page applies a separate
channel-level review for original and authentic content. It says borrowed
material must be changed significantly, describes generic or repetitive content
separately from reused content, and applies reused-content review to the channel
as a whole. Its July 15, 2025 notice renamed the former repetitious-content
policy to `inauthentic content` and stated that the reused-content policy did
not
change; that dated notice is part of the live page's update history, not a
complete version label for the current policy text.

## Repository Use And Scope

SHAR uses this record only as supporting evidence for creator guidance and the
separate legal analysis of gameplay video, streaming, and monetization. The
record does not establish publisher permission for footage from *The Simpsons:
Hit & Run* and does not authorize distribution of game files, modified builds,
extracted assets, music, dialogue, or other proprietary material.

YouTube policy and copyright analysis are independent. A creator may possess a
copyright argument but fail monetization review, or satisfy channel-originality
requirements while still lacking rights to particular game, music, or other
third-party content.

## Authorship, Ownership, And Attribution

YouTube publishes the reviewed platform policies. Copyright and other rights in
game footage, music, performances, marks, creator commentary, and uploaded video
remain with their respective owners subject to applicable licenses and law.

Attribution, a disclaimer, Content ID status, monetization approval, or the
absence of a takedown does not establish publisher authorization or fair use.

## License Or Terms Basis

YouTube's game-content monetization guidance routes commercial-use questions to
the publisher's license. It does not itself grant rights in a video game. The
channel monetization policy separately evaluates whether content is original and
authentic, including whether borrowed material has been changed significantly
and whether reused content includes meaningful commentary, substantive
modification, or educational or entertainment value.

The reused-content policy is expressly separate from copyright enforcement,
permission, and fair use. Platform monetization acceptance is therefore not a
legal adjudication, and legal permission does not guarantee monetization.

## Distribution, Modification, And Compatibility

A gameplay video should use only the amount of game material needed for its
commentary, instruction, criticism, review, or demonstration purpose. Long
unedited footage, cutscenes, soundtrack material, or dialogue can create both
rights and monetization risk, particularly when the upload substitutes for the
original entertainment experience.

The creator should retain the game-copy acquisition record, publisher policy or
permission, project files showing original commentary and editing, the access
date for YouTube policies, and evidence identifying any licensed third-party
music or other separately controlled content.

## Compliance Posture

- Verify the exact publisher's current commercial-use policy before upload.
- Keep commentary or instruction visibly tied to the material shown.
- Distinguish copyright analysis from channel monetization review.
- Do not represent Content ID status or monetization approval as legal
  permission.
- Preserve dated evidence of publisher permission, editing, commentary, and
  platform rules.
- Re-review YouTube policy before consequential publication or monetization.
